Local-first

Your photos don't go through our infrastructure.
Because we have none.

This is not a privacy policy promise. It is an architectural fact. There is no server to send your photos to.

Where your photos actually live

When you use Raza Photos, your photos stay exactly where they are — in whatever folders you already have on your Mac. Raza Photos reads them there and indexes their metadata into a local database. Nothing is copied, nothing is uploaded, nothing is moved.

When you browse your library on an iPhone or iPad, the photos are served directly from your Mac over your home Wi-Fi. Your phone is talking to your Mac, not to a data center somewhere.

When your Mac is off, there is nothing to connect to. That is not a bug. That is the system working exactly as designed.

The honest tradeoff: backup is your responsibility

We will say this plainly

Cloud storage services include offsite backup as part of what you pay for. Raza Photos does not. If your Mac's hard drive fails and you have no backup, you could lose your photos.

This is the one real cost of local-first storage. We think it is worth being honest about it rather than pretending it does not exist.

That said, good backup solutions for local photo libraries are straightforward and many are either free or already included with your Mac:

  • Time Machine — Apple's built-in backup to an external drive. Set it up once and it runs automatically. Your photo folders are included by default.
  • External hard drives — A second copy on a drive kept elsewhere (a drawer at work, a relative's house) is simple offsite backup.
  • NAS devices — A network-attached storage device at home gives you redundant local copies without a subscription.
  • Selective cloud backup — Tools like Backblaze or Arq let you back up specific folders to cloud storage at a fraction of the cost of a photo subscription, without giving any app access to browse or process your library.

The 3-2-1 rule is a sensible baseline: three copies of your data, on two different types of media, with one copy offsite. Time Machine plus one external drive satisfies this.

What you actually own

When your photos are in iCloud or Google Photos, you are a tenant. The service can change its pricing, its terms, or shut down entirely. Companies get acquired. Storage tiers change. In 2011, Google said Google Photos would be free forever. In 2020, they changed their mind.

When your photos are on your own drive, you are the owner. Raza Photos could disappear tomorrow and your photos would be completely unaffected — they are still in the same folders, in the same formats, readable by any software that opens JPEG or HEIC files. We deliberately use open metadata standards so that your organisational work — your albums, your face tags, your location data — lives in the files themselves, not locked in a proprietary database.

The subscription model creates a dependency. You pay not to access the service, but to keep access to your own memories. We find that arrangement difficult to justify.
