Burst navigation

Two directions.
Two meanings.

Swipe left or right to move through your library. When you're on a burst, swipe up or down to step through the frames. No menus, no mode switches — the gesture tells you where you are.

← Previous photo
Next photo →
↔

Horizontal — your timeline

Swiping left or right always moves through your photo library in time. One swipe, one photo. Every photo in your library is one swipe away from the next, including bursts — the burst appears as a single entry in the timeline.

↕

Vertical — inside a burst

When the photo you're looking at belongs to a burst, swiping up or down steps through the frames of that burst. You stay in the same moment in time — you're just drilling into the depth of it, frame by frame.

Why this way?

A burst is a single moment captured many times. It makes sense to navigate it on a different axis than time — because it isn't time moving forward, it's the same instant repeating. Keeping bursts off the horizontal axis means you never accidentally swipe past 40 nearly-identical frames to get to the next photo.

The photos peeking in from the left and right tell you you're in the timeline. The photo visible below tells you there's a burst here. The whole layout speaks before you touch anything.

Automatic grouping

Bursts are detected and grouped automatically when you import a folder. No tagging, no manual work. The app reads the burst identifier from the photo metadata and groups the frames silently.

One photo in the grid

In the photo grid, a burst appears as a single thumbnail — the first frame, with a small indicator. You won't see 40 copies of the same shot side by side.

Frame counter

While navigating a burst, the current frame number and total frame count are shown so you always know where you are inside the sequence.
