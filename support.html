Apple requires apps to ask your permission before they can listen for Bonjour announcements or connect to devices on your local network. This is a privacy protection — it prevents apps from silently scanning your network in the background. When Raza Photos asks for local network access, it is asking for exactly the permission it needs to find your Mac, and nothing more. Modern routers broadcast on both 2.4GHz and 5GHz simultaneously but keep them on the same network segment. Devices on different bands can still find each other via Bonjour — band is a radio frequency choice, not a network boundary.
