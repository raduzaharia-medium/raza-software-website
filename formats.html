Supported formats

If your iPhone can open it,
Raza Photos can too.

We use Apple's own frameworks to read and display your photos and videos. The supported format list is Apple's, not ours — which means it stays current automatically as Apple adds new formats.

Photos

Your iPhone shoots HEIC. Your archive is probably JPEG. Both are first-class citizens. For everything else, if Photos.app can open it, so can we.

.heic · .heif HEIC / HEIF

Apple's default format since iPhone 7. Half the file size of JPEG at the same quality, full HDR support, and where your Live Photo still lives.

.gif GIF

What about RAW?

DNG, CR2, ARW, NEF, and other camera RAW formats are not supported. Raza Photos is built for your photo library — the files your iPhone and Photos.app produce — not a professional RAW workflow.

Videos

The video your iPhone recorded plays. The movie you downloaded from the internet probably won't — and that's intentional. Raza Photos is not a media player.

.mov QuickTime MOV

Apple's native video container. Every iPhone video recording since the beginning. Supports HEVC, H.264, ProRes, and spatial video.

iPhone default
.mp4 · .m4v MPEG-4

The universal video container. The same codecs as MOV in a more portable wrapper. Works everywhere Apple's framework runs.

Not a movie player

MKV, AVI, WMV, and other formats used for downloaded movies or video editing are not supported. If a format is not natively handled by Apple's AVFoundation framework, it will not play.

HDR

On a capable display — iPhone 12 and later, MacBook Pro with Liquid Retina XDR, Pro Display XDR — your HDR photos and videos appear exactly as bright and vivid as when you captured them.

The gain map trick

HDR photos are not stored as HDR. Each file contains a standard SDR image — which looks perfectly normal on every screen — plus a hidden gain map: a set of instructions for how to boost specific areas when shown on a capable display.

On a standard screen, the gain map is ignored. On an HDR screen, it lights the photo up to its full captured brightness. One file, two presentations, every screen looks correct.

Raza Photos hands the complete file — gain map and all — directly to Apple's display framework. We decode nothing. The display does the work.

Live Photos

Live Photos are not really a file format — they are two files that belong together. Here is the honest explanation of how that works.

What a Live Photo actually is

When you take a Live Photo, your iPhone captures 1.5 seconds of video before and after the shutter moment, alongside the still. Inside Apple Photos you see one item. On disk it is always two separate files: a HEIC for the still and a MOV for the motion, linked by a shared identifier hidden in the metadata of both.

To keep that link intact when exporting, use File → Export Unmodified Originals in Apple Photos. A regular export produces only the still.

📤

Export from Apple Photos

File → Export Unmodified Originals gives you two files per Live Photo: IMG_1234.HEIC and IMG_1234.MOV, side by side in the same folder.

🔍

Raza Photos reads both

When it imports the folder it reads the shared Live Photo identifier from the HEIC's Apple MakerNote metadata and from the MOV's QuickTime content identifier. Files with a matching ID are recognised as a pair.

🔗

The pair is linked automatically

The MOV disappears from your grid — not shown as a separate video. The HEIC becomes a Live Photo. Tap or hover and the motion plays, exactly as it does in Apple Photos.

✅

Nothing to configure

No special folder structure, no naming convention required. As long as both files share a folder and the metadata identifier is intact, pairing is automatic.

If it plays as a still

The MOV companion is probably missing. This happens when you copy only the HEIC, or export without Unmodified Originals. Re-export using File → Export Unmodified Originals and import again.
